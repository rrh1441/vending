01 — The Opportunity

A market that cannot clear itself.

Demand for premium indoor tennis in Seattle has outrun supply for a generation. Nobody is building the answer. We are.

The Seattle Tennis Club has turned members away for years behind a reported eight-to-ten-year waitlist at a ~$30,000 initiation — a price set by legacy and held there by a member-owned board with no reason to let the market clear. Tennis Center Sand Point serves more than 4,000 members on ten courts. Every premium court in the city is spoken for.

Meanwhile more than 150 new pickleball courts are opening across the region — and not one new premium indoor tennis club. The capital chasing racquet sports is pointed at the cheaper game. The most affluent, most underserved players in the city have nowhere to go.

This is the rarest thing in consumer real estate: enormous, durable, pre-existing demand and zero incoming supply. The buyer doesn't need to be created. They are already standing in line somewhere else.

02 — Why Now

The price has already been proven next door.

Six blocks from our target corridor, the East West Club is selling $25,000 founding memberships for a golf-simulator concept — a narrower sport, a smaller audience, the same affluent buyer. They are proving, in real time and in our exact neighborhood, that Seattle will pay club-level initiations for a modern, design-forward members' experience.

Tennis commands a larger audience than golf simulators, a deeper bench of juniors and families, and an incumbent with an eight-year line out the door. If $25,000 clears for golf bays, our economics are conservative by comparison.

An eight-year incumbent waitlist is not equilibrium — it is unmet demand we capture on day one.

03 — The Club

Built to be the institution the city is missing.

Eight indoor courts engineered to championship specification, with two-to-three fitted with connected ball-tracking technology — shot analytics, video, and app-integrated coaching that no Seattle club offers today.

Around the courts: a members' lounge and bar, conditioning and fitness space, locker rooms, a pro shop, and viewing galleries. A purpose-built clubhouse for tennis, designed for the household and the junior player as much as the competitor.

The technology is the hook the city has never seen; the membership is the product. Court access is bundled into dues — no meters running, no hourly friction. You belong, and you play.

Why a founder pays the premium.

The founding seat is not more tennis — it is a permanently scarce asset. Dues locked at a standing 20% discount for life. Full transferability, so the membership appreciates with the club. A capped "1 of 100" status no future check can buy. A governance seat and first call on prime court time.

The locked discount costs the club roughly $135K a year across all hundred founders — and the founder premium over a standard seat recovers it in under three years. The most loyal members in the club pay us first, evangelize hardest, and stay longest.

The riskiest dollar in any build is de-risked before you commit.

Roughly half is funded by membership initiations — pre-paid, non-dilutive customer revenue collected before opening, not investor capital. Members buy a membership, not a stake; the equity and the ownership remain entirely with the investors who fund the balance.

The land problem becomes a partnership.

Suitable sites are scarce and ground-up construction is required — so we don't buy the dirt, we partner for it. A landowner contributes a parcel into the project as equity rather than selling it, converting Seattle's tightest constraint into an aligned partner and removing the single largest cash cost from the build.

Conservatively financed, with real upside.

On conservative dues realization the club covers its debt service with room to spare; as the member mix matures, coverage and margin climb materially. The lean ground-lease structure is what keeps the leverage modest and the downside protected.

07 — The Platform

One club is the proof. The platform is the prize.

We are not building a club. We are building the operating company that builds clubs.

A brand-and-operating Platform Company owns the design template, the technology stack, the member-acquisition engine and the team. Each club is a separate Project Company, financed deal-by-deal: member initiations, a landowner partnership for the site, and modest project debt.

The flagship's founding campaign and stabilized economics become the live proof that makes the platform fundable. Then we recycle real-estate capital through sale-leaseback into the next site — and the next. The eventual portfolio of stabilized buildings is precisely what institutional real-estate capital pays a premium to own.

A premium racquet club has something a developer dreams of: customers who pay, in full, before the doors open.

09 — Risk Factors & Mitigants

The honest ledger.

Every real opportunity carries real risk. We have identified ours plainly and built the plan to absorb them. Each is paired with the specific mitigant already designed into the structure.

Demand realizationSelling 270 top-tier seats at full dues
The entire economic upside rests on member mix. Mitigant: a 90-day refundable-deposit campaign at full price points serves as the go/no-go gate before any site is committed — we prove the demand before we spend the capital.
Initiation pacingA slow founding-member raise
A pre-opening shortfall would strain the stack. Mitigant: a committed equity bridge from one or two lead investors backstops the campaign, and founder deposits are refundable if the club fails to open — de-risking the buyer and disciplining our timeline.
Site & zoningScarce land, Seattle permitting
Suitable parcels are limited and the city's industrial-zone rules are intricate. Mitigant: the landowner-JV structure turns the scarcest input into a partner; a land-use attorney and sports-facility architect confirm permitting at pre-application, before commitment.
ConstructionCost & schedule on a ground-up build
Ground-up construction carries cost and timing exposure. Mitigant: a guaranteed-maximum-price contract with an established Seattle general contractor, locked at design development, with owner and contractor contingencies carried in the budget.
Single-asset returnOne club is a lifestyle business
A standalone club generates strong cash but not a venture multiple. Mitigant: the platform architecture is designed from day one to scale and to exit the stabilized real estate at institutional multiples — the flagship is the proof, not the whole thesis.
StructuringTwo classes of securities
Raising platform equity and project equity alongside member deposits is legally intricate. Mitigant: experienced fund-formation and real-estate-syndication counsel structures the entities and disclosures before any capital is accepted.
